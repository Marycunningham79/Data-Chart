South Bronx NYCHA Residences Get An Energy Boost

Energy efficiency scores for NYCHA buildings in the South Bronx are higher compared to last year.

By: Mary Cunningham

November, 16 2022

It’s that time of year again: Over 20,000 buildings across New York City just received new energy efficiency grades that will be publicly displayed for all to see.

The ratings, required under Local Law 33, are based on buildings’ water and energy consumption. Grades A to D represent numeric values, whereas F indicates a building's failure to submit benchmarking information.

One sector that saw improvement was NYCHA’s developments in the South Bronx.

According to Rochel Leah Goldblatt, deputy press secretary for NYCHA, the agency is working to reduce their carbon footprint. Two developments in the South Bronx are undergoing geothermal updates and four are undergoing domestic hot water updates.

When asked about how the South Bronx will continue to improve its energy efficiency ratings, Goldblatt replied, “Our intent is to follow the decarbonization map, which NYCHA updates every six months. We also provide detailed updates to NYCHA’s Sustainability Agenda progress."
